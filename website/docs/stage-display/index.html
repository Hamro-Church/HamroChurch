Stage Display

Run audience, stage, and livestream outputs with confidence.

Hamro Church supports multiple presentation surfaces. This guide focuses on the practical setup steps for outputs, stage displays, and OBS-oriented workflows.

Best for

Projection leaders and stream operators

Use this guide before your first public service on a new machine or whenever you redesign the output setup.

Outputs

Outputs are the screens or destinations Hamro Church controls. Start by identifying your main audience display, then add stage, stream, or confidence views only after the main path is stable.

  • Label screens clearly on the operating system.
  • Assign the audience output first.
  • Test media playback on the actual destination display.

Stage view

Stage view should show only what musicians, preachers, or service leaders need. Keep it readable from distance and avoid overloading the layout with unnecessary operator details.

Lower third layouts

Use lower thirds when you want scripture or announcements on a stream without replacing the full video feed. Rehearse typography, safe areas, and how bilingual text fits inside the chosen layout.

OBS and livestream control

Hamro Church can be part of an OBS workflow when the team clearly separates who controls presentation and who controls the stream. Use repeatable scene layouts and test transitions before service day.

OBS tip: Keep one rehearsal checklist for lower thirds, stage notes, and media playback so the stream operator is not troubleshooting live.

Testing checklist

Before every live event, verify the output routing, lower-third behavior, stage view readability, and any audio or video handoff needed for the stream machine.

  1. Play a video and confirm the right screen receives it.
  2. Trigger a scripture lower third and review the crop.
  3. Check stage timers and notes from the stage position.
  4. Restart once before the event if the machine has been used heavily that day.
